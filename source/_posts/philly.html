We watched the sub 500 Miami Marlins take on the bottom feeding Phillies for what promised to be a less than enthusiastic match up. The Phillies got off to an early lead and continued to punish the fish for the remainder of the evening. So much so, George and I decided that after good 7th inning stretch we depart for the bar.

The night escalated. Russell, a friend from Seattle now calls Philly home encouraged us to meet him at a nightclub mere blocks from the Liberty Bell. I could see George's shoes inside and promptly began to pound on the door to no avail. The apartment was on the second floor, and I remembered seeing a fence I could climb up onto the balcony. My arm got barked up by the fence, and my shoes ripped, but I had successfully mounted the balcony. Here I proceeded to rap rhythmically on the patio door with no signs of stirring. My attempts for entry ceased.

George has no cell phone, I can't make any noise, and my key is lodged in the front door. Fortunately (I guess you'd call it that) there was a relatively comfortable patio chair, where I spent my final night in Philly. Once I finally woke George not a word was spoken, and we left for Canada in a sleep deprived, disgruntled fashion.
